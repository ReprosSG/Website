The Judicial Framework

Canada's criminal justice system operates within a constitutional framework that guarantees the rule of law, the separation of powers, and the protection of individual rights under the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms. According to the World Justice Project Rule of Law Index 2024, Canada ranks 12th out of 142 countries globally and 10th out of 31 in the North America and Western Europe region — reflecting a high-performing but not flawless system, with persistent gaps in civil and criminal justice accessibility.

The federal government's approach to criminal justice emphasises rehabilitation and reintegration alongside public safety. The 2024 State of the Criminal Justice System Report highlights the Youth Criminal Justice Act as a notable success: youth crime rates have declined significantly over the past two decades, and community supervision has increased. Correctional Service Canada (CSC) runs Intensive Rehabilitative Custody and Supervision (IRCS) programmes, as well as Section 81 Healing Lodges designed to respond to the distinct needs of Indigenous offenders — a priority area given that Indigenous people remain disproportionately represented in Canada's prison population.

With an intentional homicide rate of 1.94 per 100,000 population (Statistics Canada / UNODC, 2023) — a 14% decrease from 2022 — Canada demonstrates a downward trend in violent crime despite persistent challenges in certain urban and Indigenous communities. Gang-related homicides accounted for approximately one-quarter of all homicides in 2023, representing an ongoing public safety concern that the federal government is actively addressing through its Federal Framework to Reduce Recidivism.

Corruption & Accountability

Transparency International's 2024 Corruption Perceptions Index places Canada at its lowest-ever ranking in the 30-year history of the index, scoring 75 out of 100 and falling to 15th position — a drop of three places and one point from 2023. Transparency International Canada attributes this decline to structural weaknesses in lobbying transparency, beneficial ownership disclosure, and foreign interference accountability, which have eroded public confidence in federal institutions.

Despite this concerning trend, Canada remains firmly in the top tier of the global ranking and continues to benefit from an independent judiciary, a free press, and robust anti-corruption legislation. Transparency International Canada calls for legislative reform — particularly around strengthening the Lobbying Act, expanding public registers of beneficial ownership, and reinforcing the independence of oversight bodies — to reverse the downward trajectory and restore Canada's standing as a global benchmark for clean governance.
