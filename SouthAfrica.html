The Judicial Framework

South Africa operates a hybrid legal system combining English common law, Roman-Dutch civil law (inherited from the Dutch Cape Colony and entrenched during British colonial rule), customary law of indigenous African communities, and the transformative constitutional framework of the 1996 Constitution — widely regarded as one of the most progressive constitutional documents in the world. The judiciary is structured across Magistrates' Courts, the High Court (with divisions in each province), the Supreme Court of Appeal in Bloemfontein (the highest court for non-constitutional matters), and the Constitutional Court in Johannesburg — established as the supreme apex court over all matters following the 17th Constitutional Amendment of 2012. The Constitutional Court, housed in the acclaimed Constitution Hill complex on the site of the former Number Four Prison where Nelson Mandela and Mahatma Gandhi were once incarcerated, sits as the guardian of the 1996 Constitution and the Bill of Rights — the most comprehensive human rights catalogue in Africa.

South Africa's Constitutional Court has been internationally recognised for its transformative jurisprudence. In 2025, President Ramaphosa appointed Justice Nonkosi Dambuza-Mayosi and Justice Rosheni Allie Savage to the Constitutional Court bench — the first Muslim woman and the first woman from the Northern Cape appointed to the court. The court's 2025 docket was dominated by electoral and governance disputes arising from the Government of National Unity (GNU) coalition formed after the May 2024 general election — in which the ANC lost its parliamentary majority for the first time since 1994 — as well as landmark rulings on Section 25 land expropriation, digital rights, and the constitutionality of the National Health Insurance Act. South Africa's judiciary remains widely respected for its independence despite deep institutional pressures arising from the state capture era.

South Africa recorded 27,621 murders in the 2023/24 financial year — a rate of 44.9 per 100,000 population, a 20-year high — according to official SAPS crime statistics. This makes South Africa one of the most violent countries in the world outside active war zones. Firearms were used in 42% of murders; 2,886 were gang-related in the Western Cape alone. The 2024/25 data shows the first meaningful improvement in years: Q3 (Oct–Dec 2024) recorded 6,953 murders — a 9.8% year-on-year decrease — and the full-year 2024/25 annual total fell to approximately 24,692, a 10.6% decline, according to PoliceData ZA. Q4 2025 (Jan–Mar 2025) recorded 5,727 murders — 62 per day. Violence is geographically concentrated: Gauteng and KwaZulu-Natal account for 46% of all murders; the Western Cape records the highest gang-related murder rate, concentrated in Cape Town townships including Bishop Lavis, Mitchells Plain, and Delft.

Corruption & Accountability

South Africa scores 41 out of 100 on the Transparency International Corruption Perceptions Index 2025 (published February 2026) — unchanged for the third consecutive year, confirming a score of 41 in 2023, 2024, and 2025. The country ranks 81st globally, and sits below the global average of 42 for the first time as that average itself declined. Cape Argus notes that South Africa ranks only 12th among sub-Saharan African countries — behind Rwanda, Botswana, Namibia, Cape Verde, Ghana, Senegal, Ivory Coast, and Benin — a ranking incongruent with its status as the continent's most industrialised economy. The stagnation is attributed by Corruption Watch to the incomplete dismantling of the state capture networks exposed by the Zondo Commission (2018–2022), and to continued failures to prosecute senior officials implicated in looting state entities including Eskom, Transnet, and PRASA.

South Africa's anti-corruption ecosystem includes the Directorate for Priority Crime Investigation (Hawks), the Special Investigating Unit (SIU), the National Prosecuting Authority's Investigating Directorate Against Corruption (IDAC), and civil society organisations including Corruption Watch. Despite the Zondo Commission recommending hundreds of prosecutions, the NPA has made limited progress: as of 2025, fewer than 30 individuals from the state capture network have been convicted, out of an estimated 1,438 persons implicated. Former President Jacob Zuma — who served 15 months of a contempt-of-court sentence in 2021 — had his arms deal corruption trial (Zuma vs State, ongoing since 2005) again delayed to 2025, though a 2024 Constitutional Court ruling definitively upheld his right to stand trial. The GNU coalition under President Ramaphosa, formed in June 2024, has renewed anti-corruption commitments — but institutional capacity and prosecutorial independence remain constrained.

Institutional Landmark
Constitutional Court — Constitution Hill, Johannesburg

La Cour constitutionnelle d'Afrique du Sud, inaugurée en 2004 sur le site de Constitution Hill à Braamfontein — là où Nelson Mandela, Mahatma Gandhi et des milliers de militants anti-apartheid furent emprisonnés — est le tribunal le plus haut du pays et le gardien de la Constitution de 1996. Sa façade distinctive porte son nom gravé dans les onze langues officielles de la nation arc-en-ciel. Conçue par les architectes Urban Solutions et OMM Design Workshop, elle est construite avec des briques récupérées des anciennes prisons — un acte délibéré de mémoire et de transformation.
